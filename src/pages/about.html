About MiTHAI

Reimagining how we document culinary creativity.

The Story

"Mithai" means sweets in Urdu. MiTHAI was created to solve a common kitchen problem: recipes are rarely static. Ingredients are swapped, cooking times adjusted, and new flavors tested—but these changes often go undocumented.

Unlike most recipe apps, MiTHAI treats recipes as living documents. Users can create "Remixes" (variations), track which version worked best, and see how a dish evolves over time.

What MiTHAI Does

MiTHAI helps home cooks, food bloggers, and meal preppers track their culinary experiments in a structured and meaningful way. Instead of relying on scattered notes or memory, users can store original recipes and every variation they create.

  • Create base recipes with ingredients and instructions
  • Build “Remixes” that record only the changes
  • Rate recipes and variations over time
  • Visualize cooking data through charts and insights
  • Export or clear stored recipes when needed

Every base recipe becomes a starting point, and every remix is linked back with clear change logs. Over time, users can see how a dish evolves — what worked, what didn’t, and which version became a favorite.

Why It Matters

Cooking is both creative and experimental, but many successful tweaks are never written down. MiTHAI solves this problem with a simple goal: Keep the good ideas — and learn from the rest.

  • Avoid repeating mistakes
  • Reference their best versions
  • Improve dishes through data and reflection
  • Share reliable results with confidence

Recipes become living documents, evolving just like the people who cook them.

Features at a Glance

  • Full recipe and remix creation
  • Image upload & API images
  • Ratings and average scores
  • Search and filtering by cuisine, meal type, and ingredients
  • Guided cooking mode
  • Charts showing ingredient usage and recipe statistics
  • Local data storage that persists between sessions

Designed to be responsive, accessible, and intuitive, MiTHAI works on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Technology

  • HTML5
  • CSS3 and Bootstrap
  • JavaScript
  • LocalStorage
  • TheMealDB API (for recipe images and inspiration)
  • Chart.js (for visual insights)

The application follows WCAG AA accessibility guidelines, ensuring clear structure, keyboard navigation, and high contrast.
